Steak Recipe

Description

Cooking the perfect steak can be the most painful experience for the unexperienced. It looks easy, but sometimes it can be intimidating for a beginner. Today, we will be learning how to prepare for the meal during all stages. From shopping for an affordable but tasty chunk of protein all the way to cleaning tips. Look forward to learning about a student's experience on how to cook the perfect steak.

Prerequisite Ingredients

Before we get started:

  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Butter or Oil (whatever you prefer)
  • Cooking Pan
  • Cooking Utensils (Ex. Tongs)

Preparation Steps

  1. Pick out steak of choice (For beginners, I would recommend something without a bone like Sirloin Steak)
  2. Put steak in freezer with packaging of choice (I would recommend Ziploc bags) (If you plan on cooking the same day, skip Steps 2 & 3)
  3. Defrost steak at least a few hours before cooking (Tip: If you forgot to do this, place packaged steak into a bowl of warm water)

Steak Rarity

Before cooking, pick out a steak rarity that you want to aim for, the less you let the steak cook, the rarer/juicier the steak will be.

Cooking Steps

  1. Preheat pan to a medium-high heat
  2. Allow your butter or oil to preheat by placing it onto the pan
  3. While waiting for the pan to heat, preseason the steak with salt
  4. To test if the pan is ready, wet the tips of your fingertips with water and flick some into the pan. If the water drops sizzle and jump around, the pan is ready.
  5. Place your steak into the pan and allow it to sear for 3-5 minutes (or until your liking)
  6. Once that side has a nice char to it, flip the steak to the other side. Allow it to sit for another 3-5 minutes
  7. Once the steak has finished cooking, place the steak onto a plate and let it rest for at least 5 minutes to retain the juice.
  8. While letting the steak rest, take a paper towel and wipe the remaining stuff off of the pan (with tongs) to help reduce time spent cleaning.
  9. Wash and dry your pan and clean the cooking surfaces of the oils generated from cooking.
  10. Enjoy your steak!
